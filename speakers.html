Allan Kilavuka

Group Man. Director & CEO, Kenya Airways





Allan Kilavuka

CEO, Kenya Airways



Allan Kilavuka serves as the Group Managing Director and CEO of Kenya Airways Plc and holds non-executive board positions in its subsidiaries, Jambojet and Precision Air. With over 23 years of leadership and management experience, Allan is highly experienced in initiating and managing organizations, facilitating change, financial planning, integration, process improvement, and ensuring regulatory compliance.
Before joining KQ, Allan played a pivotal role in the turnaround of GE Power Services Africa and GE Industrial Solutions, based in South Africa. His career began at Deloitte East Africa in Nairobi, specializing in audit, accounting, outsourcing, and custodianship. He holds a Bachelor of Commerce Degree from the University of Nairobi, a Postgraduate Certificate in Psychology from the University of Liverpool, and has received advanced training in executive leadership, advanced management, and financial planning at GE's Crotonville Leadership Institute in New York.

Abderahmane Berthé

Secretary General at African Airlines Association (AFRAA) Kenya.



Abderahmane Berthe is the Secretary General of the African Airlines Association (AFRAA). Before joining AFRAA, he served as Chief Executive Officer for Air Mali and Air Burkina. Hawkins Musili

General Manager – Fahari Aviation





Hawkins Musili

General Manager – Fahari Aviation



Hawkins is an experienced engineering and innovation expert with over a decade of experience in project and team management at Kenya Airways (KQ). He is a founding member of Fahari Aviation, a subsidiary of KQ, where he currently serves as the General Manager.

Fahari Aviation is a leader in the use of Unmanned Aerial Systems (UAS) in Kenya, with applications in infrastructure inspections, surveillance, agriculture, mapping, research, conservation, and logistics.Before his current role, Hawkins was the Head of Engineering Services at KQ, where he oversaw continuous airworthiness, maintenance cost management, asset management, fleet acquisition, disposal, powerplant engineering, OEM and vendor negotiations, engine cost analysis, and asset management. He holds an MSc in Aeronautical Engineering with a specialization in Aircraft Powerplants.

Mr. Mesfin Tasew

Group Chief Executive Officer – Ethiopian Airlines





Mr. Mesfin Tasew

Group Chief Executive Officer – Ethiopian Airlines



Mr. Mesfin Tasew was appointed as Group Chief Executive Officer of Ethiopian Airlines in March 2022. He joined Ethiopian in 1984 as Associate Engineer and progressed through the ladder and served in supervisorial and managerial positions in the Aircraft Maintenance and Engineering areas. In 1997 and 1999, he was appointed as Director Operations & Technical Systems Support and Chief Information Officer, respectively. In 2006, he was appointed as Vice President of Maintenance and Engineering. Mr. Mesfin also served as Chief Operating Officer of Ethiopian Airlines from 2010 – 2021, and Chief Executive Officer of ASKY Airlines from April 2021 to March 2022. Mr. Mesfin also serves as a member of the International Air Transport Association (IATA) Board of Governors. He holds BSC and MSc Degrees in Electrical Engineering from Addis Ababa University. He also holds Master’s in Business Administration (MBA) from Open University in the UK.
